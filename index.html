Mind

How an individual talks to themselves, what habits influence their actions and how we can reprogram our brains. Body

We'll help you understand the history and origins of these practices, and then fully equip you with the techniques to reintroduce yourself to your body. Whether exercise or inner-peace is your goal, we'll teach you what suits your body and mind requirements.

CPR Meditation

Inhale: “I am calm.”

Exhale: Feel it.
Inhale: “I am peaceful.”
Exhale: Feel it.
Inhale: “I am relaxed.”
Exhale: Feel it.

shoulders

Body Scan

Slowly move your attention throughout

your body, beginning at your feet and
working your way up toward your head.
Observe any areas holding tension.

Square Breathing

Inhale to 4,

Hold to 4,
Exhale to 4,
Hold to 4.

Inhale: 4, Exhale: 8

Inhale to 4,

Exhale to 8.

Walking Meditation

Repeat this as you walk in a circle:

I have arrived
I am home
In the here
In the now
I am solid
I am free
In the ultimate
I dwell

Moving Meditation

Practice a form of yoga, tai chi, or qi

gong that connects your breath with
movement. Notice your sense organs response.
Every inhale and exhale, change your body's position.

Self Inquiry Meditation

(as taught by Sri Ramana Maharshi)

Ask yourself, “Who am I?”

Guided Meditation

Try following a guided meditation on

YouTube. You can also use apps such

Mantra Meditation

On each exhalation, chant a mantra

such as OM or Shanti. (Shanti translates
to mean peace.)

Candle Meditation

Light a candle and focus your gaze on

the flickering flame.

Smile Meditation
